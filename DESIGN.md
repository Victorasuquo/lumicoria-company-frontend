# Lumicoria.com Design Direction

## Design read

A premium B2B company site for founders, operators, and enterprise leaders evaluating an AI delivery partner. The experience is transparent, editorial, calm, and focused on business value before technical detail.

## Dials

- Design variance: 7.
- Motion intensity: 5.
- Visual density: 4.

## Reference synthesis

- Kore.ai: confident category framing, enterprise trust, and platform proof beneath a simple promise.
- Vapi: human language, strong media-led hero, immediate product demonstration, and clear enterprise readiness.
- Retell AI: large framed visual moments, a short outcome-led headline, product proof, trust, integrations, and FAQs.

The implementation borrows the shared UX strategy, not the brands, layouts, copy, or visual assets of the reference sites.

## Visual system

- Space Grotesk for display and body, with Space Mono reserved for labels and operational signals.
- Light neutral canvas with restrained violet, blue, and mint atmospheric color.
- Transparent surfaces use soft borders, material blur, and solid-fill fallbacks for reduced-transparency preferences.
- Large editorial typography, framed visual moments, asymmetrical supporting layouts, and intentional dark sections establish rhythm.
- Cards appear only when they contain a distinct decision, service, or interaction.
- Motion supports orientation and state change. Reduced-motion preferences are respected.

## Page architecture

1. Plain-language hero and business-day visual.
2. Production proof strip.
3. Interactive outcome explorer by business function.
4. Company story and Lumicoria.com plus Lumicoria.ai relationship.
5. Three-step delivery approach and six-week production path.
6. Transparent engagement options and pricing ranges.
7. Trust, human control, and applied research.
8. Buyer FAQs.
9. Direct discovery call close.

## Voice

- Lead with business results and recognizable work.
- Explain technical depth only after relevance is established.
- Use confident, straightforward language without hype.
- Do not invent customers, testimonials, certifications, or performance claims.
